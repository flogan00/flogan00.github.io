Research

General interests

As I mentioned before, I am an MMUF fellow, where I conduct research about labor issues and the analytic revolution, especially as it pertains to youth baseball organizations, specifically the Amateur Athletic Union (AAU), and Little League Baseball (LLB). I am also interested in neoliberal politics within sports broadcasting from the 1970s to 2020s.


Current projects

Right now, I am researching pitch count, the worth of pitcher accolades (such as perfect games, no-hitters, complete games, etc.), pitcher health, and capitalism.


Researching style

I was brought up in my early undergraduate as a classical studies scholar, so archival research is very much within my wheelhouse, using multi-media sources. I'm moving towards a more sociological style of research and writing and have been working on an IRB to interview youth baseball players. I'm a firm believer in academic accessibility, so I do my best to make my presentations and papers as friendly to the widest audience I can through detailed explanations and references, as well as staying true to my primary goal, which is to be a storyteller first and a scholar second.
